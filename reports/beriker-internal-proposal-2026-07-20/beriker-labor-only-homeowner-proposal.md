## Scope By Phase

### 1. Preconstruction

Includes site verification of existing dimensions, slab elevations, beam/post conditions, carport drainage, existing electrical/HVAC access, and finish tie-in conditions; plan review; inspection sequence coordination; and clarification tracking before work begins.

### 2. Mobilization And Protection

Includes labor to set up the work area, protect the occupied home, protect the porch/carport approach and nearby finishes, provide dust control, create temporary separation at affected openings, and maintain daily cleanup.

### 3. Selective Demolition

Includes labor to remove only the walls, siding/trim, doors, casework, and affected components shown for removal on the plans; preserve remaining carport elements intended to stay; and load debris for disposal.

### 4. Foundation / Raised Floor / Slab Interface

Includes labor to verify existing slab/foundation conditions, layout and build the raised floor/foundation interface per plan details, install anchors/dowels/blocking/venting where required, coordinate required inspection points, and maintain drainage/slope at affected areas.

### 5. Exterior Framing / Building Shell

Includes labor for new exterior wall framing below the existing carport roof/beam, sheathing installation, weather-resistive barrier, flashing, sealants, siding and trim tie-ins, and carport-facing fire separation backing where required.

### 6. Windows / Exterior Doors / Glazing

Includes labor to install owner-approved exterior French doors and windows, sill pans, flashing, casing, trim, hardware, and related weatherproofing. Door/window units, glass, hardware, screens, and trim materials are excluded from this labor-only price.

### 7. Electrical

Includes labor allowance for required dining room receptacles, lighting, switches/dimmers, exterior/weatherproof/GFCI items affected by the work, smoke/CO updates required by inspection, and standard rough/finish electrical labor.

### 8. HVAC / Appliance Rough-In

Includes labor allowance for basic register extension or relocation, refrigerator location coordination, minor appliance rough-in coordination, and patching related to labor routes. HVAC equipment replacement or redesign is excluded unless added by written change order.

### 9. Cabinet / Kitchen Tie-In

Includes labor for refrigerator cabinet/end panel modifications, paint-grade tie-in carpentry, and finish carpentry around the new access/opening. Full kitchen cabinet replacement, countertop replacement, backsplash replacement, and appliance purchase are excluded.

### 10. Insulation / Drywall / Fire Separation

Includes labor for insulation installation, required fire separation between carport and living space, gypsum board installation, fire caulking, drywall hang/tape/texture, and affected tie-in areas.

### 11. Interior Finishes

Includes labor for installing interior trim/casing/base, paint labor at new and disturbed surfaces, flooring labor, transitions, finish hardware installation, and punch-related finish touchups. Flooring, paint, trim, hardware, and finish materials are excluded.

### 12. Exterior Concrete / Drainage / Weatherproofing

Includes labor allowance for exterior landing/slab touch-up directly affected by the new door/opening work, slope adjustment immediately adjacent to the new work where practical, and weatherproofing closeout at exterior wall bases/openings.

### 13. Closeout

Includes final cleanup labor, punch list completion, inspection closeout coordination, and photo documentation of concealed fire separation, flashing, and foundation conditions where useful.

## Owner-Provided / Excluded From Labor-Only Price

- All lumber, sheathing, fasteners, anchors, epoxy, concrete, vapor barrier, WRB, flashing, siding, trim, gypsum board, insulation, fire caulk, sealants, flooring, paint, cabinet materials, doors, windows, glazing, hardware, electrical devices/fixtures, HVAC parts, registers, appliance parts, refrigerator/freezer, and finish materials.
- Permit fees, plan revision fees, design/engineering fees, agency fees, and special inspection fees.
- Disposal/dump fees unless added separately.
- Hazardous materials testing/remediation, asbestos, lead, mold, rot, termite damage, or hidden damage.
- Electrical panel/service upgrade.
- Major HVAC redesign or equipment replacement.
- Major plumbing reroutes outside the directly affected area.
- Full kitchen remodel, full countertop replacement, full cabinet replacement, or backsplash replacement.
- Exact match of aged/discontinued siding, trim, flooring, cabinetry, paint, stain, hardware, or textures.
- Broad concrete slab replacement, drainage redesign, landscaping, irrigation, roofing, gutters, or hardscape work beyond directly affected areas.

## Change Order Triggers

Written change order will be required if existing conditions differ materially from the plans, if the slab/foundation cannot be reused as anticipated, if the inspector requires additional fire separation or code upgrades, if electrical/HVAC routing is not accessible, if owner selections require added labor, if owner-furnished materials are late/defective/incomplete, or if hidden damage/nonconforming previous work is discovered.
