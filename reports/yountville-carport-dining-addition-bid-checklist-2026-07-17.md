# Yountville Carport-to-Dining Addition Bid Checklist

Project: 2006 Webber Ave, Yountville, CA  
Owner shown on plans: Melissa Beriker  
Plan set: James George Designs, dwelling addition plans, plan check revisions dated 2025-02-11  
Bid purpose: Contractor scope and pricing checklist for converting part of the existing carport into a conditioned dining room addition, with adjacent kitchen/refrigerator work and owner-furnished doors/windows.

## Bid Guardrails

- Confirm bid is based on the approved permit set, not preliminary drawings.
- Confirm all plan sheets, revisions, Title 24 forms, CalGreen checklist, and mandatory measures are included in the pricing set.
- Include only the work shown for the partial carport conversion, new dining room, refrigerator/cabinet modification area, new landing, and directly affected tie-ins.
- Treat all existing conditions as field-verify before fixed price: dimensions, framing, foundation support, carport posts/beams, drainage, electrical capacity, concealed utilities, existing finishes, and door/window rough openings.
- Owner-furnished doors/windows are install-only unless otherwise agreed in writing.
- Any defective, late, mis-sized, unlabeled, damaged, or non-code-compliant owner-furnished item is a change order.
- Include written RFI/change-order process before deviation from approved plans.

## 1. Preconstruction / General Conditions

- Mobilization, site setup, layout, protection, and daily cleanup.
- Protect existing occupied residence, kitchen, living areas, exterior finishes, drive/walk surfaces, landscaping, railings, and remaining carport.
- Dust control and separation between work zone and existing living area.
- Temporary weather protection at openings and partially demolished areas.
- Coordinate owner access, parking, deliveries, dumpster/trailer location, and staging.
- Verify permit status, inspection sequence, and whether any city/county special requirements apply.
- Verify plans bear designer signature and governing agency approval before construction.
- Review all sheets and report conflicts before starting.
- Maintain jobsite safety, temporary shoring if needed, and Cal/OSHA-compliant access.
- Include administrative time for inspections, sub coordination, owner meetings, and finish approvals.

## 2. Site / Drainage / Erosion Control

- Install erosion control measures shown, including straw wattles if required by inspection/weather.
- Maintain trash and recycling sorting area.
- Protect existing drainage swale and site drainage paths.
- Confirm finish grade slopes away from new foundation: 5% for first 10 ft where feasible, or approved alternate drainage.
- Confirm impervious surfaces within 10 ft slope away from foundation at minimum 2%.
- Include backfill in lifts with clean material free of organic debris.
- Include new 3 ft x 6 ft concrete or paver landing at exterior door unless excluded or owner-selected.
- Slope exterior landing away from building at 1/4 in per ft.
- Verify finished floor height and threshold/landing code clearances.

## 3. Demolition / Selective Removal

- Remove existing carport components only where shown for conversion.
- Remove existing 3636 sliding window and keep existing opening where shown.
- Open affected wall/ceiling/floor areas needed for framing, electrical, insulation, and tie-ins.
- Protect existing carport portion that remains.
- Protect existing roof, fascia, gutter, siding, trim, concrete, railings, water heater, AC units, security camera, EV charger, and adjacent kitchen/living finishes.
- Do not remove structural members unless shown or approved by designer/engineer.
- Include careful cutting at existing finishes to allow clean patching and tie-in.
- Include debris loading, haul-off, recycling documentation, and disposal fees.

## 4. Foundation / Raised Floor / Structural

- Field verify existing slab/grade/foundation conditions at carport.
- Build new foundation/stem wall/raised floor system per plan and details.
- Confirm engineered fill/compaction requirements if any fill supports new work.
- Include excavation, forms, rebar, anchor bolts, hold-downs, post bases/caps, concrete, cure time, stripping, and backfill.
- Include PT mudsill, rim board, floor joists, blocking, plywood subfloor, and underfloor insulation.
- Include required Simpson hardware, including HDU2/SSTB16 hold-downs where shown.
- Maintain existing 4x10 carport beams/headers/posts where shown.
- Add new 2x6 exterior wall framing at 16 in OC where shown.
- Include plywood shear, panel edge nailing, blocking, and required fastening schedules.
- Include MSTA36 straps at beam splices where shown.
- Verify existing carport post bearing and wall base plate details.
- Include temporary shoring if existing support is disturbed.

## 5. Exterior Walls / Weatherproofing / Siding

- Install new board-and-batten siding to match existing.
- Include fiber cement or approved siding, building paper/wrap, Z-flashing at horizontal joints, caulking/sealants, and manufacturer-required clearances.
- Include battens approximately 3/4 in x 2 in at 16 in OC, aligned with studs or backed as required.
- Include new corner, vertical door/window trim, lintel trim, and sill trim to match existing.
- Provide owner samples/mockups for siding, trim, batten layout, and paint color before install.
- Include window/door flashing sequence per details: sill flashing, side flashing, head flashing, continuous sealant, non-hardening sealant, and galvanized metal flashing where required.
- Include door sill pan/flashing and isolation where aluminum contacts concrete.
- Exclude replacement of existing roofing, fascia, gutter, siding, and trim except where directly disturbed, unless added by change order.

## 6. Doors / Windows / Glazing

- Install owner-furnished new exterior doors/windows shown on plan, including new 3068 opening, 6068 French door, and two 3050 casements mulled together if these match the final approved set.
- Verify rough openings, handing, swing, threshold heights, egress/safety glazing, U-factor, SHGC, and Title 24 labels before installation.
- Include blocking, shimming, fastening, flashing, insulation, interior/exterior trim tie-in, and sealants.
- Confirm tempered/safety glazing requirements.
- Exclude warranty on owner-furnished units, glazing, hardware, finish, factory defects, or shipping damage.
- Change order for resizing openings, reframing, delayed units, missing parts, incorrect sizes, unlabeled units, or manufacturer-required corrections.

## 7. Roofing / Attic / Fire Separation

- Existing asphalt composition roof to remain as-is unless disturbed by required work.
- Existing rafters and ceiling joists generally remain as-is.
- Do not reduce existing attic ventilation; verify venting is maintained.
- Install radiant barrier over added conditioned living space where required.
- Maintain 1 in air space at radiant barrier/insulation.
- Include fire separation between garage/carport and living space as shown.
- Fully line required walls/ceilings with gypsum board separation where required.
- Fire caulk/fire-resistive joint systems at penetrations and joints.
- Install T&G / bead-board ceiling finish over gypsum board to match existing carport/front porch ceiling.

## 8. Insulation / Energy / Title 24

- Wall insulation: R-15 at existing 2x4 exposed stud bays and R-21 at new/existing 2x6 stud bays where required.
- Attic insulation: minimum R-38 at new attic/ceiling areas and affected existing conditioned spaces.
- Underfloor/crawl space insulation: minimum R-19 at new raised floor joist bays.
- Include radiant barrier at roof sheathing above added conditioned space.
- Include Title 24-compliant windows/doors with labels in place for inspection.
- Include required high-efficacy lighting, controls, and exterior fixture requirements.
- Alternate price: upgrade existing attic insulation to R-49 if owner wants it.
- Alternate price: insulate existing crawl space between existing girders if accessible and owner wants it.

## 9. Electrical / Lighting / Low Voltage

- Electrical is design/build by installer within code and plan intent.
- Verify existing panel capacity, breaker spaces, AFCI/GFCI requirements, and circuit routing before fixed price.
- Include new dining room lighting/switching shown on plan.
- Include relocation of existing light fixture where shown.
- Include exterior waterproof light fixtures/fans where shown, owner-selected, wet-location rated.
- Include blocking for exterior wall-mounted fixtures.
- Include tamper-resistant outlets, AFCI protection where required, GFCI/weatherproof outlets where required.
- Include smoke/CO alarm updates as required by permit and code.
- Include protection/reinstallation of existing security camera if disturbed.
- Existing EV charger to remain; protect during work.
- Confirm whether new refrigerator/freezer location requires dedicated circuit or receptacle relocation.
- Owner walkthrough required before wiring for final outlet, switch, and fixture locations.

## 10. Mechanical / HVAC

- Verify whether the new dining room needs heating/cooling supply, return path, duct extension, or load calculation.
- Include any required ductwork, registers, return-air accommodation, balancing, and Title 24 documentation if part of final scope.
- Protect existing AC condenser and tankless water heater.
- Cover duct openings and protect mechanical equipment during construction per CalGreen.
- Exclude major HVAC equipment replacement unless specifically included.

## 11. Plumbing / Kitchen / Appliance Area

- Verify existing plumbing lines, slopes, vents, and tie-in locations before pricing any plumbing changes.
- Include work related to new 36 in refrigerator/freezer location if final plan requires water/ice maker line, shutoff, recessed box, or cabinet modifications.
- Verify existing kitchen sink, dishwasher, range, and surrounding kitchen areas are not part of scope unless specifically included.
- Include only plumbing directly shown or necessary for the refrigerator/freezer/cabinet area unless owner adds scope.
- Exclude hidden plumbing repairs, drain/vent reroutes, water heater work, and unrelated fixture upgrades unless discovered and approved by change order.

## 12. Interior Framing / Drywall / Ceiling / Finishes

- Frame new dining room walls and affected openings per plan.
- Include gypsum board, fire-rated/separation board where required, taping, texture match, and patching at affected areas.
- Install bead-board/T&G ceiling finish over gypsum board where shown.
- Include interior casing/trim/base where affected, matched to existing as closely as practical.
- Include painting/priming of new and patched walls, ceiling, trim, siding, and exterior components.
- Include finish material submittals/samples for owner approval.
- Confirm flooring scope: new dining room flooring, transition to existing kitchen/living, subfloor prep, underlayment, and finish match/allowance.
- Confirm whether existing kitchen finishes/cabinets/counters are patch-only or larger remodel scope.

## 13. Cabinets / Refrigerator Area

- Include new 36 in refrigerator/freezer location shown on plan.
- Include refrigerator cabinet, end panels, and cabinet modifications to match existing if part of final scope.
- Verify existing cabinet manufacturer/style/species/finish and whether matching material is available.
- Include allowance if exact cabinet scope or finish match is not confirmed.
- Exclude appliance purchase unless specifically included.
- Owner to approve appliance specs before cabinet/electrical/plumbing rough-in.

## 14. CalGreen / Waste / Documentation

- Meet 65% nonhazardous construction and demolition waste diversion or approved local alternative.
- Prepare and maintain construction waste management plan if required by inspector.
- Track haul-off/recycling documentation by weight or volume consistently.
- Use low-VOC adhesives, sealants, caulks, paints, and coatings as required.
- Rodent-proof annular spaces around penetrations at exterior/bottom plates.
- Provide owner operation/maintenance documentation if required at final.

## 15. Finish Allowances To Define Before Bid Is Final

- Flooring material and installation allowance.
- Interior and exterior paint allowance.
- Door/window trim and sill material allowance.
- Exterior siding/trim material if exact Hardie/redwood/cedar selection is not decided.
- Bead-board/T&G ceiling material and finish allowance.
- Exterior light fixtures and fan allowance.
- Door/window hardware allowance if not supplied with owner-furnished units.
- Cabinet/ref panel/end panel allowance.
- Refrigerator/freezer appliance allowance if contractor is asked to supply.
- Landing finish: concrete vs paver, color, texture, and edge condition.

## 16. Exclusions / Clarifications To Put In Proposal

- Architect/designer/engineer fees unless specifically included.
- Permit fees, utility fees, school fees, impact fees, and agency fees unless specifically included.
- Surveying, soils engineering, compaction testing, special inspection, or structural engineering not shown.
- Hidden dry rot, termite damage, mold, asbestos, lead paint, hazardous materials, or concealed structural defects.
- Existing roof/fascia/gutter replacement beyond directly disturbed tie-ins.
- Existing electrical service upgrade unless required and separately approved.
- HVAC equipment replacement or major system redesign unless separately approved.
- Matching existing aged finishes exactly.
- Owner-furnished product warranty.
- Delays caused by owner-furnished material delivery, defects, wrong sizes, missing labels, or missing hardware.
- Work outside shaded/new construction areas and directly affected tie-ins.

## 17. Field Verification Checklist Before Fixed Price

- Confirm exact approved drawing revision and permit status.
- Measure existing carport opening, beam/post locations, slab/grade elevations, and finish floor elevation.
- Verify existing 4x10 beams/headers are continuous where required and posts are sound.
- Probe/check for rot, water damage, termite damage, or existing nonconforming conditions at carport.
- Verify foundation feasibility, drainage slope, and landing elevation.
- Confirm existing electrical panel capacity and route for new circuits.
- Confirm HVAC requirements for conditioned dining space.
- Confirm refrigerator appliance specs, cabinet dimensions, and utility requirements.
- Confirm owner-furnished door/window sizes, labels, handing, hardware, delivery dates, and storage location.
- Confirm finish selections and allowance levels.
- Confirm inspection expectations with local jurisdiction.

## 18. High-Risk Change-Order Triggers

- Existing carport framing, posts, beams, roof, slab, or foundation are not adequate or differ from drawings.
- Approved plans require additional structural hardware, engineering, or corrections after bid.
- Owner changes door/window size, swing, glazing, hardware, or product line.
- Door/window units arrive late, damaged, unlabeled, or not code compliant.
- Existing electrical panel cannot accept required new circuits/protection.
- HVAC load/ducting requirements exceed plan assumptions.
- Drainage or grade cannot meet required slope without extra hardscape/site work.
- Hidden damage discovered after demolition.
- Cabinet finish/material cannot be matched or existing cabinets require more extensive modification.
- Finish level exceeds allowance or mockup/sample standard.

## 19. Suggested Proposal Structure

- Base bid: permitted carport-to-conditioned dining room conversion per approved plans, with defined allowances.
- Alternate 1: upgrade existing attic insulation to R-49.
- Alternate 2: insulate existing crawl space areas beyond new addition.
- Alternate 3: premium cabinet/ref panel match package after cabinet source is verified.
- Alternate 4: upgraded exterior lighting/fan package after owner selections.
- Allowance schedule: flooring, fixtures, trim/siding specifics, cabinets, paint/finish.
- Owner-furnished schedule: doors, windows, hardware, appliance, delivery deadlines.
- Change-order policy: written approval before extra work, with labor/material markup and schedule impact.
